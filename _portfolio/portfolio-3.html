Motivation

hospitals manage hundreds of products monthly. Efficient inventory planning is critical: overstocking ties up budget and storage, while understocking can impact patient care. Understanding usage patterns over time and predicting future demand can help hospitals reduce waste, and optimize costs. I decided to do this project because there is a growing need for data-science and AI tools in healthcare.

Data

The data is sourced from the hospital dataset from the R expsmooth package, which was uploaded to forecastingdata.org() in the .tsf format. It consists og 767 time series, representing monthly product counts of medical products starting January 2000 and ending December 2007. The names of the products have been anonymised.

This graph makes it clear that february and march are outliers, as for why this is the case, my leading theory is that the jump from the low point in February to the high point in March is due to a recording quirk that causes some of the observations in February to be recorded as occuring in March instead. The fact that February is a month with an unusual amount of days, 28 or 29, could be the cause of this as well.

Data Forecasting

Now, lets use Prophet to do bulk forecasting for all the series:

In[12]:
from prophet import Prophet
forecasts = {}

for series_id, df_s in long_df.groupby('series_id'):
    # prepare dataframe for Prophet
    prophet_df = df_s[['timestamp','value']].rename(columns={'timestamp':'ds', 'value':'y'})

    # fit model
    m = Prophet(yearly_seasonality=True, daily_seasonality=False, weekly_seasonality=False)
    m.fit(prophet_df)

    # make future dataframe (next 12 months, for example)
    future = m.make_future_dataframe(periods=12, freq='ME')
    forecast = m.predict(future)

    # store forecast
    forecasts[series_id] = forecast[['ds','yhat','yhat_lower','yhat_upper']]

def plot_forecast(series_id):
    df_forecast = forecasts[series_id]
    # historical values for comparison
    df_hist = long_df[long_df['series_id']==series_id][['timestamp','value']].rename(columns={'timestamp':'ds','value':'y'})

    # plotting
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,5))
    plt.plot(df_hist['ds'], df_hist['y'], label='Historical', marker='o')
    plt.plot(df_forecast['ds'], df_forecast['yhat'], label='Forecast', color='green')
    plt.fill_between(df_forecast['ds'], df_forecast['yhat_lower'], df_forecast['yhat_upper'], color='green', alpha=0.2)
    plt.xlabel('Date')
    plt.ylabel('Usage')
    plt.title(f'Forecast for {series_id}')
    plt.legend()
    plt.show()
